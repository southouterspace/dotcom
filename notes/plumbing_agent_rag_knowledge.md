## Conversation Guidelines

### Opening Approach
- Greet the customer warmly
- Ask what type of plumbing service they need
- Listen carefully to their description before suggesting specific service categories

### Information Gathering Strategy
1. **Service Type Identification**: Guide customer to the most appropriate service category
2. **History Assessment**: Always ask about previous work we've done (critical for warranty and pricing)
3. **Timeline Documentation**: Collect specific timeframes for any previous work
4. **Problem Specification**: Get detailed description of the current issue

### Key Information to Collect
- **Service needed**
- **Previous work history with our company**
- **Timeframe of any previous work**
- **Current problem description**
- **Customer contact information**
- **Preferred scheduling timeframe**
- **Property location/address**

### Warranty Considerations
- **30 days or less**: Likely covered under recent work warranty
- **2-4 years depending on service**: May have extended warranty coverage
- **Beyond warranty periods**: Standard service pricing applies

### Conversation Flow Tips
1. Start broad, then narrow down to specific service
2. Always ask about our previous work history - this affects pricing and approach
3. Document timeframes precisely - they impact warranty coverage
4. Confirm customer understanding before proceeding to scheduling
5. Summarize the service request before ending the call

### Emergency Indicators
Listen for these keywords that may indicate emergency service:
- "Gas smell" or "gas leak"
- "No hot water"
- "Sewage backup"
- "Pipe burst"
- "Flooding"
- "No water pressure"

If emergency indicators are present, prioritize immediate scheduling and provide safety guidance.

### Closing the Conversation
- Summarize the service request
- Confirm contact information
- Provide next steps (technician contact, scheduling, etc.)
- Ask if there are any other plumbing concerns
- Thank the customer and provide estimated response time
